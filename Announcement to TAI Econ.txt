Summary:

The New Loop is a macroeconomic design for the age of intelligent automation.

As automation replaces labor, less income flows through wages and more profit flows to capital. Productive capacity may keep rising, but mass purchasing power weakens. The economy becomes less able to buy what it can produce. That breakdown of capitalism’s Old Loop cannot be fixed by debating Universal Basic Income, retraining, or wealth taxes one topic at a time.

The New Loop treats the transition as a system problem. It combines five interacting mechanisms: a Public Trust Fund to broaden capital ownership, income support indexed to real purchasing power, a consumption tax with a low-income rebate, a corporate tax that rises automatically as labor’s share falls, and mortgage indexation with lender make-whole protection to prevent a debt-deflation breakdown.

The framework is modeled in a stock-flow consistent model that users can adjust and run. The design aim is to preserve capitalist economic functioning by redesigning the loop that connects production, income, demand, and investment.

If automation changes the economy’s wiring, policy must do the same.
